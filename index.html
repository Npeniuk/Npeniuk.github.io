What to grow?

Easy Vegetables

  • Lettuce are fast-growing, cool-weather crop
  • Radishes are super quick (harvest in 3-4 weeks)
  • Green beans are productive and low-maintenance
  • Zucchini are crazy prolific in summer
  • Carrots are fun and colorful varieties are available
  • Potatoes are especially good in containers

Herbs

  • Basil needs lots of sun and pairs with tomatoes
  • Thyme are hardy and flavorful
  • Cilantro are quick growing
  • Parsley is great in many dishes
  • Mint grows rapidly

Fruits

  • Strawberries are easy and perfect in containers
  • Raspberries will spread, so give them room
  • Blueberries need acidic soil
  • Melons need space and sun but super satisfying

Flowers

  • Marigolds will repel pests like aphids
  • Nasturtiums are edible and trap pests
  • Sunflowers attract pollinators

Planning Tips

  • Grow what you eat there is no point in growing kale if you never touch it.
  • Consider your climate use your local planting calendar to find the best pants to grow.
  • Mix it up and try combining veggies, herbs, and flowers for beauty and function.
  • Start small get a few containers or a 4x4 raised bed is plenty for beginners.

Where to start?

Many first-time gardeners will be unsure how to start gardening and will just place a seed into the ground and wonder why their vegetables have not grown. I think you should follow my step by step guide on how to grow potatoes for begginers, I go over all of the nessasarey materials and instructions on how to start growing your own potatoes.

Why potatoes as seen in the what to grow guide, I list out some easy to grow vegetables and herbs for beginners. Potatoes are a very easy to grow plant that are a staple food in peoples diets. Potatoes have a high yield, they are one of the most productive crops per square foot.

They can be grown in grow bags making it easier to harvest, as you can dump the contents of the bag on the ground and find all the spuds. Another reason to grow them is their versatility in the kitchen roast, mash, fry, bake, soup, salad, etc. They also have a long storage life lasting months if stored correctly, unused potatoes can be turned into seeds for next season.
